# Display Campaigns

## What it is

Visually engaging ads (mostly responsive image/text combos) shown across the Google Display Network — millions of third-party websites and apps, plus Google-owned properties like YouTube and Gmail. Standard Display campaigns now have Smart Display's former AI capabilities folded in, so there's no separate "Smart Display campaign" type anymore — it's one unified Display campaign with adjustable AI involvement in bidding, creative, and targeting.

## When to use it

- Reaching people earlier in the buying cycle, before they're actively searching — building awareness or staying top-of-mind.
- Remarketing to people who've already visited your site/app (using your own data segments).
- Expanding reach beyond Search results when Search alone has hit its ceiling.
- Not the right choice if the goal is pure direct-response with tight control over exact placements — PMax or Search usually serve that better with less manual placement management overhead.

## How it works

### Creative
- **Responsive Display ads** are the default: you upload assets (headlines, descriptions, images, logos, videos) and Google AI assembles and resizes them to fit available ad inventory across the network, choosing the best-performing combination per impression.
- **Uploaded/static ads** are also supported (image or HTML5) for advertisers who want full creative control instead of algorithmic assembly.
- A free stock image library is available for advertisers without their own assets.

### Bidding
- Smart Bidding strategies are recommended based on stated goals; manual selection is also available.
- "Pay for conversions" option exists specifically for Display campaigns using Target CPA bidding — you're charged on conversions rather than clicks, shifting risk to Google.

### Targeting
- **Optimized targeting** is the default targeting method — it uses landing page content and other signals to find new, relevant audiences likely to convert, going beyond whatever specific audience lists you've selected.
- Manual targeting options remain available (audiences, topics, placements, demographics) for advertisers who want tighter control instead of optimized targeting.

### Timing expectations
- Settings changes (bid, budget, targeting) can take 12–24 hours to fully apply on the Display Network — don't judge a change's impact same-day.
- **Learning phase**: 5–7 days for new campaigns or campaigns with significant setting changes. Frequent changes during this window reset learning and delay optimization. Allow 7–14 days of stability after a change before evaluating performance.
- Reactivating a long-paused campaign also restarts the learning phase.

## Common pitfalls (audit checklist)

- **Judging performance too early** — inside the 5–7 day learning window, or within 12–24 hours of a settings change.
- **Optimized targeting left on by default without checking whether the landing page content is actually representative** of the intended audience — if the landing page is thin or mismatched, optimized targeting will reach the wrong people.
- **Thin responsive asset sets** — too few headline/image/logo variations limits how well Google AI can assemble high-performing combinations; this is the most common root cause of weak Display CTR/conversion rate that looks like a targeting problem.
- **"Pay for conversions" eligibility/setup overlooked** when a client specifically wants conversion-based risk-sharing rather than CPC.
- **No remarketing/data segment layer** — Display is often most efficient as a remarketing channel, and campaigns built purely for cold prospecting tend to underperform without that layer.
- **Frequent settings churn** during the learning phase, often from well-intentioned but premature optimization attempts.
