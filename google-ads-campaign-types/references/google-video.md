# Video Campaigns

## What it is

Video ads on YouTube and Google video partner sites/apps. Spans everything from 6-second bumper ads to skippable in-stream ads to YouTube Home Feed mastheads — usable for both brand awareness and direct-response goals depending on subtype.

## When to use it

- You want video ads on and off YouTube and have (or can create) video creative.
- Recommended for advertisers of all experience levels — setup and targeting mechanics mirror other Google Ads campaigns once you have a video uploaded to YouTube.

## Ad formats available within Video campaigns

- **Skippable in-stream ads**: play before/during/after other videos; viewer can skip after 5 seconds.
- **Non-skippable in-stream ads**: 15 seconds or less, viewer must watch the full ad — used to guarantee full-message delivery.
- **Bumper ads**: 6 seconds or less, not skippable — built for broad-reach, high-frequency brand awareness with a short memorable message.
- **In-feed video ads**: appear only on YouTube in places where people are browsing/discovering content (e.g. search results, suggested videos, home feed); clicking the thumbnail plays the video on its watch/channel page.
- **Masthead ads**: native video-based placement across the YouTube Home feed on all devices; typically reserved/booked for large-scale reach campaigns rather than bought through standard auction.
- **YouTube Shorts ads**: skippable video or image ads on the Shorts feed, optimized for the short-form mobile viewing experience, up to 60 seconds.
- Some non-skippable in-stream and bumper ads can also appear on YouTube TV depending on eligibility.

## Campaign subtypes (choose based on objective)

- **Drive conversions** (a.k.a. Video action campaigns): action-focused video ads and targeting built to drive sales/leads rather than pure reach.
- **Video reach campaigns**: maximize reach for a given budget — either reaching more unique users (via bumper ads, skippable in-stream, or a mix of both) or reaching users with your full message (via non-skippable in-stream).
- **Get views**: skippable in-stream or in-feed ads aimed at getting people to consider your product/brand — optimizes for view volume rather than conversions.
- **Ad sequence**: tells a story across a series of video ads shown to the same user in a defined order — useful for multi-step narratives (e.g., teaser → reveal → CTA).

Subtype availability and naming can shift (Google has consolidated/renamed video objectives before — "Awareness and consideration" became "YouTube reach, views, and engagements" as a marketing-objective label). If a client account shows different subtype options than expected, verify current naming via a quick search rather than assuming the list above is exhaustive.

## Targeting

- Topics, keywords, placements, demographics, audiences (affinity, in-market, remarketing) — similar audience toolkit to Display, applied to video inventory.
- Targeting via Google video partners extends reach beyond YouTube itself onto partner sites/apps that carry Google video ads.

## Setup essentials

- Requires a video already uploaded to your YouTube channel before campaign creation — there's no separate video upload flow inside Google Ads.
- Performance is tracked via views, costs, and budget detail inside the standard Google Ads reporting interface.

## Common pitfalls (audit checklist)

- **Wrong subtype for the stated goal**: e.g., running "Get views" when the actual KPI is conversions (should be Drive conversions / Video action), or running Drive conversions when the real goal is pure reach/frequency (wastes Smart Bidding's optimization on an objective that doesn't match the budget's true intent).
- **No frequency capping strategy** on reach-focused subtypes, leading to wasted impressions on the same users.
- **Bumper-only creative used for a message that needs more than 6 seconds** to land — mismatch between format constraints and creative complexity.
- **Masthead booked without enough lead time / inventory checked** — masthead is often booked in advance rather than bought at auction; last-minute requests can be a setup failure point.
- **Video assets not optimized for the placement** (e.g., horizontal-only video used where square/vertical would perform better on mobile-heavy inventory like Shorts or in-feed).
